According to the National Bureau of Statistics of China, in August 2025, the value added of industrial enterprises above designated size increased by 5.2% year-on-year[1]. On a month-on-month basis, the increase was 0.37%[1]. For the period of January to August 2025, the cumulative year-on-year growth was 6.2%[1].

Key highlights from the report include[1]:
*   **By Sector:** Manufacturing saw a 5.7% increase, mining grew by 5.1%, and the electricity, heat, gas, and water production and supply sector increased by 2.4%.
*   **By Economic Type:** State-controlled enterprises grew by 4.7%, share-holding enterprises by 6.0%, foreign and Hong Kong, Macao, and Taiwan invested enterprises by 2.3%, and private enterprises by 4.6%.
*   **High-Tech Manufacturing:** High-tech manufacturing experienced a significant year-on-year growth of 9.3% in August, and 9.5% from January to August.
*   **Key Products:**
    *   Automobile production increased by 10.5%, with new energy vehicles growing by 22.7%.
    *   Steel production rose by 9.7%.
    *   Electricity generation increased by 1.6%.
    *   Cement production decreased by 6.2%.
    *   Industrial robots saw a 14.4% increase in August, and a 29.9% increase from January to August.
*   **Sales and Exports:** The product sales ratio for industrial enterprises above designated size was 96.6%, a decrease of 0.1 percentage points year-on-year. Export delivery value nominally decreased by 0.4% year-on-year to 13029 billion yuan.

Sources:
[1] 2025年8月份规模以上工业增加值增长5.2% - 国家统计局 (https://www.stats.gov.cn/sj/zxfb/202509/t20250915_1961180.html)
